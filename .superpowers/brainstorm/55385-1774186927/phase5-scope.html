Phase 5: Which Enhancements?

Select the items you want in scope. Click multiple to build your shortlist.

1

CQT Spectrogram

Log-frequency spectrogram aligned to musical pitch. 84 bins across 7 octaves. Third tab alongside Mel and Chroma in SpectrogramViewer. Best for seeing musical pitch content — notes line up on evenly-spaced rows instead of being compressed at the bottom like mel.

2

HPSS Views

Harmonic/percussive source separation via librosa. Produces two spectrograms: one showing sustained tonal content, one showing transient/rhythmic content. Lightweight alternative to Demucs for visual analysis only. Adds ~2-3 sec generation time.

3

Onset-Strength Overlay

Transient activity curve computed from spectral flux. Can be rendered as a time-series line on the SpectralEvolutionChart, or as a semi-transparent overlay on spectrograms. Highlights kick/snare/hat attacks, arrangement transitions, and rhythmic density changes.

4

Interactive Chroma Heatmap

Canvas-rendered chroma replacing the static PNG. Zoom, scrub, and hover for detailed harmonic analysis. Reference implementation exists in sonic-architect-app's SpectralHeatmap.tsx. Shows 12 pitch classes over time with arrangement section overlays.

5

Tonnetz

6-dimensional tonal centroid features showing harmonic relationships over time. Captures fifths, minor thirds, and major thirds as continuous coordinates. Niche — most useful for harmonic analysis of melodic/chordal material. Less meaningful for percussion-heavy electronic music.
