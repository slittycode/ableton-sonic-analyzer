Phase 5 — Spectral Enhancement Mockups

What each feature would look like in the Spectral section. Synthetic data for illustration.

01 CQT Spectrogram
Low effort
Log-frequency spectrogram where each row is a musical semitone. Notes appear as evenly-spaced horizontal bands instead of being compressed at the bottom like mel. 84 bins = 7 octaves (C1–B7).
C1C2C3C4C5C6C7
02 HPSS — Harmonic / Percussive Split
Medium effort
Same audio, two views. Left: sustained tonal content (pads, bass, vocals). Right: transient hits (kicks, snares, hats). Quick visual separation without running Demucs.
Harmonic
Percussive
03 Onset-Strength Overlay
Low effort
Transient activity curve from spectral flux. Shows where attacks happen — kick hits, snare transients, arrangement transitions. Rendered as a line on the Spectral Evolution chart or as a translucent overlay on spectrograms.
onset strength overlaid on mel spectrogram
04 Interactive Chroma Heatmap
Medium effort
Canvas-rendered chroma replacing the static PNG. 12 pitch-class rows over time. Hover for values, zoom to inspect chord changes. Arrangement section boundaries as vertical markers. Reference implementation exists in sonic-architect-app.
CDEFGAB
hover for pitch energy · scroll to zoom
05 Tonnetz — Tonal Centroid
Low effort
6 dimensions of harmonic relationship (fifths, minor thirds, major thirds — each as sin/cos pairs). Shown as multi-line time-series. Useful for tracking harmonic motion and chord progressions. Less meaningful for percussion-heavy material.
